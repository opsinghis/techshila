## The Problem with Vibe Coding

We've all seen it. A developer opens their AI assistant, types a vague prompt, gets some code back, pastes it in, and hopes for the best. This is "vibe coding" — and it's how most teams are using AI today.

The results are predictable: inconsistent quality, growing technical debt, and a false sense of productivity. You're generating code faster, but you're not *delivering* software faster.

## What's Missing

The gap between vibe coding and genuine AI-powered delivery comes down to three things:

### 1. Structure
AI assistants are powerful, but they need context, constraints, and clear objectives. Without structured workflows, every interaction starts from zero.

### 2. Quality Gates
When AI generates code, who checks it? Without automated quality gates — tests, security scans, architectural compliance — you're trading one form of risk for another.

### 3. Knowledge Capture
Every AI interaction generates insights about your codebase, your patterns, and your team's conventions. Without a system to capture these, you lose them.

## The Agentic Alternative

Agentic SDLC addresses all three gaps. Instead of ad-hoc prompting, you get structured workflows with built-in quality assurance. Instead of hoping the AI gets it right, you have checkpoints and validation at every stage.

The result? Teams that adopt structured AI workflows see 40-60% improvements in cycle time — not because the AI is faster, but because the *process* is smarter.

## Getting Started

You don't need to overhaul your entire development process overnight. Start with one workflow — test generation is a great first candidate — and build from there. The key is moving from "AI as autocomplete" to "AI as a structured development partner."
